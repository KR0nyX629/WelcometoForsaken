Chapter 1: The Last Guest

Guest woke up with a start, the world around him spinning in a haze of nausea. His skull throbbed as though a hammer had struck it from within, each pulse of pain dragging him back toward the memory he wished he could forget. The explosion. It had been deafening, a roar that split the air and swallowed everything in fire and smoke. For a moment, he was certain it had ended him and the Bacon general, snuffed out his existence in a single grenade. Yet here he was, alive, though every nerve screamed otherwise. Fragments of the scene clawed their way back into his mind: the blinding flash, the shockwave that tore through the ground, the suffocating heat. He could still taste the acid taste of ash on his tongue, still feel the weight of death pressing close, as if it had brushed past him but missed its mark.
